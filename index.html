Our Mission

We believe water is the most powerful catalyst for change.

When a community gets clean water, everything shifts. Kids go back to school. Women reclaim hours they once spent walking miles to collect water. Farmers grow more food. Health improves. Economies grow.

Founded in 2006, charity: water is a nonprofit dedicated to bringing clean and safe drinking water to people in developing countries — using 100% of public donations for water programs.

Private donors and sponsors cover operating costs, so every dollar you give goes directly to funding clean water projects.

The Process

How We Work

Radical transparency — you can track exactly where your money goes.

01

You Donate

Your contribution goes into a dedicated fund. 100% is earmarked for clean water projects — operations are covered separately.

02

We Partner

We work with vetted local organizations in each country — teams that know their communities, speak the language, and understand the terrain.

03

We Build

Wells, spring protections, biosand filters, and piped systems are constructed by trained local teams using durable materials.

04

You See It

GPS coordinates, photos, and water quality reports are sent to every donor — you know exactly whose life your water touched.

Real Change

The Ripple Effect

Clean water doesn't just quench thirst. It unlocks a cascade of change across generations.

Education

Girls Return to School

Girls often spend 6+ hours a day collecting water. With a well nearby, they can attend school, study, and shape their own futures.

Health

Waterborne Disease Drops

Contaminated water kills more people annually than all forms of violence. Clean water means healthy families and thriving communities.

Economy

Time Becomes Possibility

Communities reclaim hundreds of thousands of hours each year — time redirected toward farming, business, education, and family.

Dignity

People Thrive, Not Survive

We show the "after" — not suffering, but smiling communities who now control their own futures because of clean water access.
